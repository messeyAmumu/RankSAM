Multi-Granularity 3D-Image-Text Alignment for Open-Vocabulary Tooth Instance Segmentation

Yan Tian, Long Xing, Weiping Ding
Zhejiang Gongshang University

Abstract

Instance segmentation in an oral scenario plays an important role in the dental digitization process. 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) has emerged as an effective approach to real-time 3D segmentation tasks. However, existing methods lack detailed comprehension of the scenes, which limits their ability to segment and interpret complex structures. Furthermore, it is difficult to integrate multimodal data to maintain semantic consistency across the 3D spatial and 2D image domains, degrading the quality of open-vocabulary oral instance segmentation. To this end, a multi-granularity 3D-image-text alignment approach is proposed, including a hierarchical feature distillation (HFD) module and a multimodal semantic alignment (MSA) module. HFD learns hierarchical instance features from 2D foundation models to enhance the 3D perception capability, while MSA aligns text embeddings with multi-granularity information of 3D Gaussians and their rendered instances and hierarchical maps. The experimental results on two oral segmentation datasets indicate that our approach improves multiview consistency, semantic perception, and text fidelity by a margin compared to the prevailing approaches.
